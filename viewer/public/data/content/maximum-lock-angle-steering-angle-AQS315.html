Maximum lock angle (steering angle)

The maximum angle of lock (3) is the angle which the wheels adopt at the full steering angle (left and right) relative to the axis of symmetry (2). This determines the vehicle's turning circle.

The maximum angle of lock behaves like the toe variation angle and also provides information on the status of the steering trapeze and steering mechanism.

As the adjustment of maximum angle of lock is usually only possible with off-road vehicles, this should only be checked in this type of vehicle.

The maximum angle of lock is measured with the relevant internal cornering wheel fully locked (3). Depending upon the steering trapezium, the external cornering wheel (1) must have the same negative difference, within a specified tolerance.

Example

Maximum angle of lock, right (2):

34°

Angle of lock, left (3)

32° ± 1°

Difference (1):

2°

Tolerance:

± 1°



Effects of incorrect measurement angles
  • different turning circle of the vehicle
