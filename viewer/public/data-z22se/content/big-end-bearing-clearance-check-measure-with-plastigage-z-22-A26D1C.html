Big-end Bearing Clearance, Check, measure with Plastigage) (Z 22 SE)

  Measure

1.   Oil pan removed
Note:   Oil pan removed – see operation "Oil Pan, Remove and Install".
2.   Detach the big–end bearing caps.
•   Mark the installation position of the big–end bearing cap (1).
•   Grease the big–end bearing caps and coat the big–end bearing shells with engine oil.



3.   Lay the Plastigage in place.
•   Lay Plastigage (mouldable plastic thread) across the entire width of the connecting rod bearing journal.
Warning:   Do not rotate the crankshaft.
4.   Note:
Plastigage is available via the "Opel/Vauxhall – Workshop Equipment" catalogue.
5.   Attach the big–end bearing caps.
•   Tightening torque 25 Nm + 50° + 50° + 10°
6.   Detach the big–end bearing caps.
7.   Measure the big–end bearing clearance.
•   Compare the width of the flattened plastic thread with the measuring scale.
Note:   When reading off from the scale, do not confuse millimetres and inches.
•   Permissible connecting rod bearing clearance: 0,041 to 0,043 mm.



8.   Attach the big–end bearing caps.
•   Coat the big–end bearing journals and the big–end bearing shells with engine oil.
Note:   Note the applied markings (1).
•   Use new bolts.
•   Tightening torque 25 Nm + 50° + 50° + 10°
