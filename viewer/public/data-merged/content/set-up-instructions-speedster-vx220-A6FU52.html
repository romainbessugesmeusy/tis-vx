Set-up instructions Speedster/VX220

Preparation of the vehicle

Before the chassis alignment, the following conditions must be met on the vehicle:

  • Use of prescribed wheel and tyre sizes
  • Check for perfect tyre profile. The wear picture must be even.
  • Set tyre pressures for full load. The tyre pressures must be equal on the left and right for each axle. The tyre pressure data is given in the corresponding "Service Booklet" and "Service Plan".
  • All wheels and wheel bearings must be in perfect condition.
  • There must be no apparent damage to the chassis, no play in the ball joints (tie-rod and steering joints) or suspension components.

Check camber, castor, toe and kingpin inclination and height adjustment

The following points must be performed before chassis alignment:

  • Fill fuel tank to half full.
  • Ensure that the vehicle is not loaded.
  • Park the vehicle in the set-up position. For all chassis alignments including toe, the vehicle must stand on a level surface. The vehicle must be at the same height front and rear.
  • Load the front seats as specified in the brochure "Suspension settings"
  • Check construction height. The construction height should correspond to the value given in the brochure "Suspension settings" and cannot be adjusted. On major deviations, the dampers and springs must be checked for damage and manipulation.
  • Install and adjust chassis alignment equipment on the vehicle in accordance with the manufacturer's regulations.
  • Move the steering to the straight-ahead position.

Using the electronic chassis alignment machine, establish the "actual values". For "nominal values", see brochure "Suspension settings". The notes, recommendations and instructions from the machine manufacturer must be followed.

Compile measurement report.

Adjust camber

Note:   The camber is set by inserting shims (1) of varying thickness between the control arm (2) and the steering knuckle. The shims are available in thicknesses 1 mm and 3 mm .

Raise vehicle at the front. The relevant front wheel must hang freely. Loosen both bolts (2) securing the control arm to the steering knuckle.

Pull front wheel out at the top so that shims (1) can be removed or added.

Castor, adjust

Note:   Castor is determined by arranging shims between the joints (1) and (2) of the upper wishbone. To set the castor, the upper wishbone is brought forward or set back using the shims. The total thickness of the shims - 4 x 1.5 mm - must not be changed by removing or adding further shims.

Raise the vehicle at the front.

Remove the screw connection of the front and rear joint of the upper wishbone.

The shims (1) and (3) must always be evenly divided between the front (I) and rear (II) joints.

Important:   The installation position of nitrile-coated shim (2) must not be changed. This shim must always be arranged on the back of the front control arm bush.

Toe, adjust

Important:   A toe adjustment should always be performed on both tie-rods.

Move the steering to the straight-ahead position.

Remove clamp band from gaiter. Release lock nut (1) on left and right tie-rod. Set toe by turning the tie-rod.

Tighten the locknuts on both tie-rods - 45 Nm .

Note:   Ensure that the gaiter on the steering gear is not twisted.

If necessary release clamp band, eliminate twist and refit clamp band.

Check/visual inspection

Re-check toe adjustment. The steering wheel must be in the centre position after correct toe adjustment - identifiable from test drive.

Check/set straight-ahead position

Important:   After working on the steering gear, steering column assembly, in any case check the straight-ahead position of the steering.
