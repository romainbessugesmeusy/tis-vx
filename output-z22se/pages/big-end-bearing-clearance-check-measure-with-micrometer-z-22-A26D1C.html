Big-end Bearing Clearance, Check, measure with micrometer (Z 22 SE)

  Measure

1.   Fit the internal gauge and calibrate it with the micrometer (1).
Attach the big–end bearing caps to the connecting rods.
  • Tightening torque 25 Nm + 50° + 50° + 10°

1

Measure the diameter of the big–end bearings at 3 points.
 
  • Use the inside micrometer to measure at the points I, II and III.

2

Measure the diameter of the big–end bearings at 3 points.
 
  • Use the inside micrometer to measure at the points I, II and III.

3

Measure the diameter of the big–end bearings at 3 points.
 
  • Use the inside micrometer to measure at the points I, II and III.

4

Measure the diameter of the big–end bearings at 3 points.
 
  • Use the inside micrometer to measure at the points I, II and III.

Calculate the mean value for the big–end bearing diameter.
 
  • Formula: I + II + III / 3



2.  

Determine the big–end bearing journal diameter.

1

Measure the diameter of the big–end bearing journals at 2 points.
 
  • Use a micrometer to measure at the points I and II.

2

Measure the diameter of the big–end bearing journals at 2 points.
 
  • Use a micrometer to measure at the points I and II.

3

Measure the diameter of the big–end bearing journals at 2 points.
 
  • Use a micrometer to measure at the points I and II.

4

Measure the diameter of the big–end bearing journals at 2 points.
 
  • Use a micrometer to measure at the points I and II.

Calculate the mean value for the big–end bearing journal diameter.
 
  • Formula: I + II / 2



3.   Determine the big–end bearing clearance.
•   Formula: Mean big–end bearing diameter – mean big–end bearing journal diameter
4.   Comparison between specified values and actual values
•   Permissible big–end bearing clearance: 0,041 - 0,043 mm
